# Volume 1: The Builders of the Sand

<WarningBox>
  **ERA:** Ancient Egypt, Circa 2500 BCE. **LOCATION:** The Subterranean Tunnels
  of Giza. **YOUR MISSION:** Survive the trapdoor and find the exit before the
  oxygen runs out.
</WarningBox>

You brush the sand from your eyes. The air down here is thick, smelling of crushed limestone and ancient dust.

Just three minutes ago, you were standing outside the massive, sun-baked blocks of the Great Pyramid. Now, you are trapped inside it. You took one wrong step, a stone tile gave way, and you slid down a pitch-black chute deep into the earth.

You ignite your flashlight. The beam cuts through the darkness, revealing a perfectly square chamber. But something is wrong.

_GRIND._

A massive, bone-rattling sound echoes through the room. Dust falls from the ceiling. You look up and realize the terrible truth: the stone ceiling isn't just a ceiling. It is slowly, steadily grinding its way down toward the floor. It is a compression trap.

You frantically search the walls. There are no doors. No levers. Just a single stone pedestal in the center of the room. On top of the pedestal rests a dusty, heavy clay tablet and a coiled, ancient rope with exactly twelve knots tied into it.

Carved into the wall behind the pedestal is a message in ancient hieroglyphs:

> _"The Builders leave a path for those who understand the measure of the earth. Find the true center of the floor, or be buried with it."_

You look down at the floor. It is made of massive, perfectly square stone tiles arranged in a massive grid. The ceiling groans again. It has dropped another inch. You don't have time to pry up every single tile to find the escape tunnel. You have to calculate exactly which tile hides the exit.

## The Chamber Opens

_You may only read this page AFTER you have successfully calculated the correct tile on your Puzzle Forge sheet!_

You rush to the tile marked by your calculations. It is heavy, but you wedge your fingers into the ancient grout and heave with all your might.

With a harsh scrape, the tile slides backward.

A rush of cool, stale air blasts up into your face from the dark hole below. You grab the 12-Knot rope, tie it to the pedestal, and repel down into the darkness just as the massive stone ceiling slams into the floor above you with a deafening _BOOM_.

You survived the trap. But your flashlight flickers. The beam lands on a heavy wooden door covered in strange, wedge-shaped cuneiform writing.

You aren't in Egypt anymore. You've somehow crossed into the ancient merchant vaults of Babylon...
